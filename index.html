Defining Filipino Song Genres Through Lyrics

How do words mirror Filipino Music?

Next

About songs...

Songs are relatable and expressive forms of art that help connect people with the same taste of feeling.

Because of the diversity of people’s music taste and music artists, various music genres have been formed and have risen to popularity.

Next

What is OPM?

Original Pinoy Music or OPM is one of the most popular music genres in the Philippines and is also recognized from all over the world.

Talented artists in the Philippine music industry showcase their art which are appreciated and loved by millions of people, resulting in millions of streams and views in different music platforms such as Spotify. Since the launch of Spotify's dedicated OPM hub in 2015, streams on the platform and the OPM music has risen year by year.

As of January 1, 2019, Spotify has reported 10 billion streams from the OPM genre on their platform. According to the tribunePH, the OPM music genre plays a significant role in the current mainstream music prominent in the Philippines. With the OPM hub, countless streams and sharing of OPM content have been made, which led to the rise of OPM artists such as Ben&Ben, IV of Spades, Zack Tabuldo, and much more.

Next

Motivation

Most people like listening to particular genres like OPM which is why music platforms such as Spotify and YouTube Music have made significant efforts to classify similar songs so they could recommend the right songs to their users. Oftenly, they base their recommendations on song genres. Aside from musical attributes and features, the lyrics of a song can also help identify which genre it falls under. With this in mind, we got curious and wanted to explore how we can define Filipino song genres through their lyrics.

Original Pinoy Music

We used the MusixMatch API to identify the genres to be included in the dataset. The collected data is made up of Tagalog songs from six genres: Pop, Alternative, Rock, Christian & Gospel, Hip Hop/Rap, and R&B/Soul.

We also used other existing lyrics APIs since MusixMatch only shows 30% of a song’s lyrics. For those songs whose lyrics are not found in other APIs, we just used the 30% of the lyrics provided by MusixMatch.

Based on the distribution of the graph, the result shows a positively skewed distribution in relation to the length of the songs of each music collected. Furthermore, most of the sampled songs have length of 150-200 words, but the average length of a song is between 243 to 244 words.

For this analysis, it was expected that the artist who will get the longest average length is a rapper and that happens to be Smugglaz. He along with Schizophrenia and Curse One are the top 3 artists with the longest average length of songs having around 542, 457, and 443 words respectively. It can also be seen that the average length of song lyrics per Artist is related to the average length of song per music genre, as the top 3 artists in this graph are artists that deliver Rap music, the genre that got the longest average length.

This question focuses on understanding which genre/s are most frequently paired with the main genres established in this project. To answer this question, we will again be using the CountVectorizer() function because we need to get the frequency count of each genre in the main dataset.

Based on the set of graphs, we have observed that:

  • Alternative genre is most frequently paired with Pop (44.2%) and Rock (28.7%)
  • Christian & Gospel genre is most frequently paired with Pop (26.1%) and Praise & Worship (13%)
  • Hip-Hop Genre is most frequently paired with Rap (86.2%)
  • Pop Genre is most frequently paired with Alternative (30.8%) and Rock (26.5%)
  • R&B Genre is most frequently paired with Hip-Hop and Rap(both at 27.5%)
  • Rock is most frequently paired with Pop (38.6%) and Alternative (29.1%)

What words make a song Pop? Hiphop? Rock? Etc?

Here, we tried to identify and understand which words best encompass and identify the genres being addressed by finding out the set of words that makes the song part of a specific genre. For this section, we will be using the Term Frequency - Inverse Document Frequency (TF-IDF) measure which basically ranks the most relevant terms for a given genre. In other words it is the measure of weight/importance of the word to, in our case, the specific genre being addressed. In a nutshell, the TF-IDF value shows how original the specific word is to the specific genre, therefore it can be used to determine what words define a genre.

Our key findings after analyzing the results

Certain set of words could be related to a genre

Based on the overall results of this project, it appears that certain set of words to be related to a specific songs genre or theme. For example, using vulgar or curse words are common in Rap songs while words associated to love are usually found in Alternative and R&B/Soul songs.

Next

Commonly combined genres most likely have similar word choice

From the results of the specific research questions, commonly combined genres most likely have similar word choice since most of them have same (or related) theme.

Words of particular genres could be interchanged and we would not even notice the difference because the main difference will lie on the tones and musical attributes of the songs. This is evident in Filipino songs, especially for R&B/Soul and Pop, because the themes of most songs under those genres are similar (crowd favorite theme: love/relationship).

Next

Hip Hop/Rap and R&B/Soul are similar despite their nature difference

Another interesting finding is that Hip Hop/Rap and R&B/Soul has a high lyric similarity rate despite the fact that the nature of hit songs from both genres are somehow contradicting each other—Hip Hop/Rap has a fast wordplay and may include strong language while R&B/Soul is usually slow and about love or soft emotions. Instances like this will make defining a song genre based only on lyrics a challenging task.

Results could be improved with sufficient resources and data

After completing the project, we believe that results and analysis could be further improved if sufficient data was available and more songs from different genres were collected. It would also help if more stop words and fillers were identified to get more meaningful insights.

Data Collection

We discovered the MusixMatch API which we found useful for the purpose of this project. We got the list of music genres from the MusixMatch API. The sub-genres and other irrelevant genres (e.g. instrumental music, K-pop) were filtered out since we will be working on Tagalog songs. However, MusixMatch API has its limitations for the free version

  1. Users are only allowed to fetch 100 songs at a time
  2. Only the first 30% of the lyrics were provided

Given those, we set our goal to get at least 200 songs from every genre. We minimized the number of API requests by attempting to fetch the top 201-300 songs of every genre first before retrieving the top 1-100 and 101-200 to check if it has a sufficient number of songs. If the request for the top 201-300 songs returns empty, then we will move and check a different genre. To get the full version of song lyrics, we used other existing APIs for lyrics which were Genius, AZLyrics and MetroLyrics. We opted and set the code to randomize the order of the use of the three APIs for the sending of requests so that it’s not heavy on just one API.
